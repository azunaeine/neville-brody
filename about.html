About

Neville Brody is an important typographer because of his contribution to magazines. He is most famous work is “The Face”. This platform changed the way that designers and readers perceive typography and layout. Furthermore, Neville Brody made significant contributions in designing record covers for independent record companies named Fetish, Hannibal, and the Phonogram around the 1980s. Neville Brody is such an important typographer because of his significant contribution to typography. Brody created many typefaces such as Arcadia, Industrial, and Insignia. Brody incorporated dadaism and pop art in his art pieces because they contributed to the growth of his art style. Brody's work contributed to the spread of dadaism and pop art. However, Brody has never copied or replicated pop art and dadaism in his art. He took dadaism and pop art as forms of dynamism and humanism. Brody utilized and tested his self-made san-serifs typography. His art and typography caught the attention of many music record companies like Fetish Records and Stiff Records. Brody’s designs targeted the grungy and punk scene. Brody art directed Micro-phonies by Cabaret Voltaire. Brody is notoriously famous for applying typography features on the front and a covered figure ejecting liquid from its mouth while staring at the viewer. When Brody worked for “The Face” he altered and changed the rules that were present in British culture by applying a creative and lively aesthetic. His designs caused a lot of emotions. For some people who saw his artwork, they would be on one page for a long period instead of turning to another page. Brody is such an important typographer because he made largely influenced typographers and created new typefaces in typography. Brody’s typography is widely recognized and used by many artists, creators and typographers.
