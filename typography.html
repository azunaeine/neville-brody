Typography

Brody is notably known for creating his own typography. His most famous typography is called the new wave style. This style of typography openly resists strict grid-based arrangements. This makes the design look more unique and meaningful. The spacing and layout of the letters make the design look irregular, showcasing some presence of a punk era. The text intrigued the younger generations, which was the target market for the Face magazine. His new wave style typography is widely known today and has influenced many typographers.

Brody used bold and irregular layouts for his designs and typography. Brody is also known for working in “mixed media”. Mixed media is defined as a way for someone to use different techniques and styles in a single design, by blending handmade ways with the utilization of computers. His approach to design has been widely used by designers today. There are many designers and typographers who have utilized his styles and techniques in their designs. Nowadays, designers are unafraid to utilize bold and unusual layouts in their designs or typography. Brody has influenced typography because many designers are stealing his techniques and incorporating them into their typography. Moreover, Brody created other fonts such as “insignia”. His creation of fonts has inspired other typographers to create their own fonts. Brody created a pathway for designers to be bold and ambitious when designing and to make new discoveries in design. Such as creating a new font.
